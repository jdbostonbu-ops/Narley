# Narley Vision

**Project Name:** Narley

**Project Type:** Mobile Community Resource Platform

**Current Version:** Version 3 Planning

**Pilot Organization:** Community Partners in Action (CPA)

---

# Vision Statement

Narley is a mobile-first community resource platform designed to help individuals quickly locate trusted community services while enabling verified organizations to keep resource information accurate, current, and accessible.

Narley is built on the belief that access to reliable information can directly improve lives. By combining community reporting, provider verification, mapping technology, accessibility features, and AI-assisted verification, Narley aims to become a trusted source for community resources.

Narley is designed to reduce the frustration of outdated resource directories by making resource information continuously verified through both community participation and provider oversight.

---

# Mission Statement

Narley's mission is to connect individuals with verified community resources in a simple, accessible, and trustworthy way.

The platform empowers:

- Individuals seeking assistance.
- Community organizations providing services.
- Partner agencies maintaining resource quality.
- Communities working together to improve local resource information.

---

# The Problem

Many existing resource directories suffer from several problems:

- Outdated information
- Incorrect phone numbers
- Closed organizations remaining listed
- Incorrect addresses
- Limited accessibility
- Difficult navigation
- Information scattered across multiple websites

People experiencing homelessness, reentry, financial hardship, or crisis often spend valuable time searching for resources that no longer exist or have outdated information.

Narley exists to solve this problem.

---

# The Solution

Narley combines modern mobile technology with community participation.

The platform allows:

- Readers to discover nearby resources.
- Providers to manage their own resources.
- Community members to report inaccurate information.
- AI to assist with resource verification.
- Providers to review and approve updates.

The result is a continuously improving community resource network.

---

# Reader Application

The Reader application is designed for individuals searching for assistance.

Primary goals include:

- Finding nearby resources.
- Viewing resource details.
- Saving resources.
- Receiving reminders.
- Getting directions.
- Reporting incorrect information.
- Using Emergency Mode.
- Accessing the application in multiple languages.

The Reader experience should remain simple, calm, and easy to understand, especially during stressful situations.

---

# Provider Application

The Provider application allows verified organizations to manage resources.

Providers can:

- Create resources.
- Update resources.
- Pause resources.
- Archive resources.
- Review community reports.
- Verify AI recommendations.
- Maintain accurate resource information.

Providers remain the authoritative source for published resource data.

---

# Community Intelligence

Narley introduces Community Intelligence as a core feature.

Community Intelligence combines:

- Community reports.
- Provider review.
- AI-assisted verification.

Readers help identify problems.

AI gathers supporting evidence.

Providers make the final decision.

This approach encourages community participation while maintaining data quality through human verification.

---

# AI Philosophy

Artificial Intelligence supports providers but never replaces provider decision making.

AI may:

- Detect outdated resources.
- Verify business status.
- Check disconnected phone numbers.
- Identify possible closures.
- Estimate confidence levels.

AI never:

- Deletes resources.
- Closes resources.
- Changes provider information.
- Publishes changes automatically.

Final authority always belongs to verified providers.

---

# Accessibility

Accessibility is a core design principle rather than an optional feature.

Narley emphasizes:

- Simple navigation.
- Large touch targets.
- High contrast colors.
- Plain language.
- Multi-language support.
- Icon-based navigation.
- Map-first design.
- Reader-friendly layouts.

The application should remain usable by individuals with varying literacy levels, disabilities, and technology experience.

---

# Design Philosophy

Narley is designed around five principles.

## Trust

Users should feel confident that resources are accurate.

## Simplicity

The application should require minimal learning.

## Accessibility

Everyone should be able to use Narley regardless of language or reading ability.

## Community

Readers, providers, and partner organizations work together to improve information quality.

## Reliability

The application should prioritize stability, performance, and dependable information over unnecessary features.

---

# Pilot Partner

The initial pilot organization for Narley is:

Community Partners in Action (CPA)

CPA will provide feedback regarding:

- Resource categories.
- Provider workflows.
- Community reporting.
- Resource verification.
- Future feature priorities.

Narley will evolve based on CPA recommendations rather than assumptions.

---

# MVP Philosophy

Version 3 focuses only on features required for the initial CPA pilot.

Future features remain documented but are intentionally excluded until requested or approved by CPA.

This approach keeps development focused while allowing future expansion.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Narley is intended to become a sustainable platform that communities can use to maintain accurate resource information.

Future goals include:

- Expansion to additional nonprofit organizations.
- Expansion to additional cities and states.
- Improved accessibility features.
- Additional provider partnerships.
- Enhanced AI verification.
- Community collaboration tools.
- Data insights for partner organizations.

Expansion should always preserve Narley's core mission of providing trustworthy community resource information.

---

# Success Metrics

Narley will be considered successful when:

- Readers can quickly locate nearby resources.
- Providers can easily maintain accurate information.
- Community reports improve resource quality.
- AI reduces manual verification effort.
- CPA successfully uses the platform during the pilot.
- Resource information remains more accurate than traditional static directories.

---

# Guiding Principle

Every feature added to Narley should answer one question:

**Does this make it easier for someone to find accurate help when they need it?**

If the answer is no, the feature should be reconsidered.

---

# Vision Summary

Narley is more than a resource directory.

It is a community-driven platform that combines verified organizations, community participation, accessibility, and AI-assisted verification to create trustworthy, up-to-date resource information for those who need it most.

Every design decision, technical decision, and future enhancement should support this mission.
